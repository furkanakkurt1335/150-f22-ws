Workshop Question 6: Recursion - The Perrin's Skiponacci Numbers

Please read important information first.

Description

You are supposed to read an integer `n` from a file. Then, you are expected to write a recursive function under the definition of `skiponacci` given to you. A Skiponacci number is the sum of two Skiponacci numbers the second and the third before. In Perrin's Skiponacci Sequence; First number: 3, Second number: 0, Third number: 2. The `skiponacci` function eventually returns the `n`th Skiponacci number. Note: Using recursion is mandatory; other solutions are not accepted.
Input Output
file1.txt 
Result: 5
Count: 5
file2.txt 
Result: 68
Count: 97
file3.txt 
Result: 10
Count: 13
