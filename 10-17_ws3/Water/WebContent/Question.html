Description

We will use a square prism-shaped containers which has a height container_height , square base edge container_base_edge , a cylindrical glass which has a base radius glass_base_radius , height glass_height , and the number of students student_count for this question.

Read the five variables container_height, container_base_edge, glass_base_radius, glass_height, student_count from a given file name, each placed in a separate line, respectively.

Write three functions calculate_container_base_area, calculate_container_volume, and calculate_glass_volume.

calculate_container_base_area must calculate the container's base area as container_base_edge2 and return that value.

calculate_glass_volume must evaluate the volume of the glass by 3 * glass_base_radius 2 * glass_height, taking the PI as 3.

calculate_container_volume must evaluate the volume of the pot by container_base_edge2 * container_height .

There are containers for drinking water in the form of square prisms at various points at Boğaziçi University. Students drink water from these containers using cylindrical glasses. There are X students in the school. One student drinks 10 glasses of water a day from these containers.

How many liters of water does each student drink per day?

These containers are filled with water once a day. How many containers are needed for enough water for all students?
